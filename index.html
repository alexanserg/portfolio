About me

Having moved around quite a bit, I found Portland is my favorite place. Few places can compete with the outdoor activities available here, or the amenities available in the city.

Restaurants

  • The Duckhouse
    • This is one of the finest chinese restaurants I personally have had the opportunity to experience. There is a soup there called "Bamboo Fungus Soup." I didn't even know that was a thing, untill I had it here, and it is delicious.
  • K-Town
    • Korean BBQ is quickly becoming very popular, and they do it right at K-Town. The meat selection is great, and their variety of panchan (or side dishes) is hard to beat.
  • Angel Food & Fun
    • This small mexican restaurant has a variety of dishes that are far from the standard tacos and burritos. The Cochinita de Pabil is good enough to give you goosebumbs

Fishing

  • Crap fishing on the oregon coast.
    • Few kinds of fishing offer even beginners the opportunity to catch their daily limit. With the daily limit being 12 dungeness crab and 24 red rock crab, a $10 daily license and affordable rental gear is enough to catch a delicious seafood fest and have fun while doing it. Oregons beautiful coast is not to be missed either.
  • Salmon and steelhead fishing
    • Oregon is home to one of the largest salmon and steelhead runs in the country. While more challenging than crabbing it is a great way to enjoy Oregons fabulous rivers and forests. You may have many trips where you catch nothing, but you are sure to enjoy yourself never-the-less.

Camping and hiking

  • Oregon is home to some 202,000 acres of wilderness. From the coast to the desert and everything in between, there are a lifetime worth of opportunities to get back to nature.
    • Three sisters wilderness
      • Located in the central Oregon Cascades, Sisters wilderness offers nearly unmatched pristine beauty.
    • Multnomah Falls
      • Located in the Columbia Gorge, any trip to the Portland Area would be incomplete without visiting this stunning waterfall.
